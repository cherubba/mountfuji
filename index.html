Meet Fuji-san! 富士山

Mount Fuji, Japanese Fuji-san, also spelled Fujisan, also called Fujiyama or Fuji no Yama, highest mountain in Japan. It is a volcano that has been dormant since its last eruption, in 1707, but is still generally classified as active by geologists. The mountain is the major feature of Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park (1936), and it is at the centre of a UNESCO World Heritage site designated in 2013.

Fuji-san has become famous throughout the world and is considered the sacred symbol of Japan.

Come visit the Fuji Museum!

Fuji Art Museum (東京富士美術館, Tōkyō Fuji Bijutsukan) was established by Daisaku Ikeda and opened near the Sōka University campus in Hachiōji, Tokyo, Japan, in 1983.

Featuring over 30,000 works of art from Japan and around the world, the museum is well-known for its collection of Western oil paintings covering 500 years of history. Other treasures include paintings, prints, photography, sculpture, ceramics and lacquerware, and armor, swords and medallions.
